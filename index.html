Database Management System (C++) repo 09/2011 Ôºç 12/2011

This is a complete database management system with limit functionalities, as a course project for CS222 at University of California, Irvine. It includes Page File Management (pf), Record Management (rm), Index Management (ix), and Query Engine (qe). For more detailed information, please refer to project reports in Reports directory. This is a cooperation workpiece with Changjian Zou. It is for personal reference only and should not be copied or referred by any others without notice.

Profile Recorder (Lua Javascript) repo circa 04/2005

An add-on built for the game World of Warcraft to record inventory information and display it offline. It was once a pity for players in mainland China to consistently fall behind on the game update compared to the rest of the world. While WoWArmory was online for players on all servers but those in China, I resolved to build a profile recorder and browser on my own. I was pretty happy with this and shared with friends who also played World of Warcraft at that moment.

Made in China

Born in Cangzhou, Hebei, a small city in northern China, I grew up with other kids whose parents were from different regions of the country. Studying and living in Hangzhou, Zhejiang for about 10 years granted me a special view of and bond with people from so-called Jiangnan. I am not sure which region I belong to, but I am quite clear that I love this land.

Life in the States

Denver, Colorado

Sunny days, dry, hiking, snow in May. These are some keywords popping in my mind when I recall the days I lived and worked there. It was not Denver exactly but cities around, even Colorado Springs. With company of co-workers / friends, it was not too bad even though we typically needed to spend about one hour on commute every day.

Irvine, California

Arriving in California the first time, I was impressed by the strong sunshine here -- which I had heard too much from different sources, despite I had been in Denver for a few times. There is only one season and it is a nice one, warm and sunny. Yeah, there are occasionally a few days too hot each year, but they are way more manageable even without a fan; you may need airconditioning in winter though. Coming back and forth between the department and campus, I could not believe I was one of the students at University of California, Irvine in the first couple of months. But it was true -- I earned it, with tremendous effort. Only I knew how much I had spent for this, and I am proud of it.
